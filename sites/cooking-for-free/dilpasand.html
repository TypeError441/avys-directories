EQUIPMENT
  • Mixing Bowls
  • Fry Pan
  • Baking Tray
  • Oven
INGREDIENTS
  • SWEET DOUGH
    • 2 cups All Purpose Flour
    • 1/2 cup Warm Milk
    • 1 tsp Active Instant Dry Yeast
    • 2.5 tbsp Sugar
    • 1.5 tbsp Butter at room temperature
    • 2 tsp Oil
    • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • SWEET COCONUT STUFFING
    • 1 cup Fresh Grated Coconut
    • 1/2 cup Tutti Frutti
    • 4-5 Candied Cherries Finely Chopped
    • 8-10 Whole Cashews
    • 8-10 Almonds
    • 1 tbsp Melted Butter
    • 1 tbsp Finely Powdered Sugar
    • 1/2 tsp Cardamom Powder
INSTRUCTIONS
  1. In a small mixing bowl add 1 tsp active instant dry yeast, 2.5 tbsp sugar and 1/2 cup warm milk. Mix well and let it stand for 5 mins until the yeast is activated.
  2. In a mixing bowl add 2 cups all-purpose flour & 1/4 tsp of salt. Mix it well.
  3. Pour the yeast mixture and roughly mix it with a spoon. Add 1.5 tbsp butter and 2 tsp oil.
  4. Knead it until the dough is soft, smooth and springy.
  5. Let it rest for 1-2 hours in a warm place until it has doubled up in volume.
  6. Meanwhile, prepare the sweet coconut filling – dry roast 1 cup fresh grated coconut in a pan until it is warm and slightly toasty.
  7. Add it to a mixing bowl with 1/2 cup tutti frutti, 4-5 finely chopped candied cherries, 8-10 each of roughly broken cashews and almonds with 1 tbsp melted butter.
  8. Now add 1 tbsp sugar finely powdered along with 1/4 tsp cardamom powder.
  9. Mix everything well together.
  10. Now punch down the doubled-up dough and cut it into two parts equally.
  11. On a baking sheet, roll a part of dough about 7 inch dia of about 1/2 cm thickness.
  12. Place the prepared sweet coconut mixture at the center, spreading it evenly leaving about 1/2 an inch gap at the edges.
  13. Now roll the other part of the dough slightly thinner and larger than the first part. I rolled it about 10” in dia.
  14. Carefully place it over the top of the filling.
  15. Close & seal the edges tightly.
  16. Let the prepared bun raise for about 30-45 mins in a warm place and let the dough raise again.
  17. Meanwhile preheat the oven at 170°C for 20 mins. Once the bun has almost doubled up, make a slit at the center using a sharp knife/blade. Brush the dough with milk.
  18. Bake at 170°C for 20-25 mins until it is golden brown both at top and bottom. Remove from oven.
  19. Once cooled down, cut into slices of desired size. Serve warm or cold with a cup of coffee/tea.
NOTES
  1. Choice of add-ons are as per preference. The cookie wrapper I saved up had mentioned dried papaya, cranberries, almond flakes, coconut flakes, raisins and chocolate chips.
  2. I had liquid glucose lying around since a couple of months and when I saw that the ingredients contained that, I gladly used it up. But taste wise, it was just like sugar. Honey can be used if liquid glucose is not available.
  3. If the dough is a little sticky, grease your palms with extra butter.
  4. Chop up the raisins if they are too big (like mine were), the raisins bulged and changed color as I had put them whole. Helps equally distribute add-ons.
  5. The cookies would be soft to touch when just out of oven, let them cool down for 5 mins before removing them from the tray for further cooling.
